How to Join

Everything you need to know about becoming part of Truckee Snowbotics:

Who Can Join

Any student at Tahoe-Truckee High School is welcome to join, along with incoming freshman in middle school. No prior robotics experience required.

Meeting Times - Season

Meetings are held Mondays and Fridays after school from 2:30-4:00 PM in M5. Any updates will be posted in respective channels. Build Season is approximately September to February.

Meeting Times - Post Season

Post-season meetings are held on Mondays after school in M5 for planning and preparation for the next season along with fun activities.

How to Apply

Fill out the interest form linked below. A team captain will follow up with next steps. You can also email us at thsrobotics@ttusd.org or come to a meeting in person.

What is FTC?

FIRST Tech Challenge — robotics competition for students in grades 7–12.

The Challenge

Each season, FIRST releases a new game in September. Teams design, build, and program a robot to compete in a specific game objective, with matches played on a 12×12 ft field.

The Season

The build season runs from September to January. Teams compete at qualifier tournaments, then advance to regional championships, and top teams qualify for the World Championship.

Skills Developed

Students gain hands-on experience in mechanical engineering, electrical systems, programming (Java/Kotlin), CAD design, project management, and teamwork.

More Than Robots

FTC awards points not just for on-field performance but also for engineering documentation, outreach, and gracious professionalism — FIRST's core value of competing with integrity.

For Sponsors

Businesses and individuals who sponsor Snowbotics may be eligible to deduct their contribution as a charitable donation. We can provide donation receipts and documentation upon request.

All funds go directly toward robot parts, competition registration fees, travel to tournaments, tools, and educational materials for team members.
